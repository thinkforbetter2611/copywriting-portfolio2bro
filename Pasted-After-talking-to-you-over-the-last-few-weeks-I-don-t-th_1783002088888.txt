---

I'd give Replit this prompt:

---

# PROMPT

> Design a premium editorial portfolio website for a copywriter. Avoid generic portfolio layouts, templates, glassmorphism, gradients, neon effects, or startup aesthetics.
>
> The overall feeling should resemble a cross between an A24 companion book, Monocle magazine, Kinfolk, British publishing houses, Resident Evil 4's menu system, Christopher Nolan's restrained typography, and a designer's creative notebook.
>
> The interface should feel tactile, cinematic, and sophisticated rather than flashy.
>
> The visual language must be extremely minimal, but filled with tiny moments of delight that reward interaction.
>
> Build the experience around storytelling instead of projects.
>
> Each campaign should feel like turning a page in an editorial book.
>
> Use generous white space, elegant typography, restrained colour palettes, museum-like layouts, and subtle asymmetry.
>
> Every animation should feel intentional and almost invisible.
>
> Avoid anything that feels like a landing page or SaaS website.

## Interaction language

Create interactions such as:

* buttery smooth scrolling
* slightly delayed typography reveals
* text that gently fades upward while scrolling
* images revealing through masks
* subtle parallax (very restrained)
* soft hover movements (2–4 pixels)
* cursor that slightly magnifies editorial details
* page transitions that resemble turning a printed page
* text that changes weight or colour on hover instead of dramatic scaling
* slow fade between projects
* cinematic loading sequence lasting under one second
* smooth easing everywhere
* no bouncing animations
* no flashy effects
* no particle systems

## Typography

Typography is the hero.

Large editorial headlines.

Small notebook-like annotations.

Elegant hierarchy.

Perfect spacing.

No decorative fonts except perhaps tiny notebook labels.

## Colour palette

Warm white

Off-white

Charcoal

Muted lavender

Dust blue

Forest green

Cream

No saturated colours except where artwork naturally introduces them.

## Layout

Every project is a chapter.

Every chapter has its own rhythm.

Some pages are almost empty.

Some are image dominant.

Some are quote driven.

Never repeat the same layout twice in succession.

## Micro-interactions

Include tasteful details such as:

* page numbers updating while scrolling
* tiny progress indicator
* image captions fading in
* smooth underline animations
* images expanding very slightly on hover
* notebook annotations appearing after the main content
* ambient paper shadow
* extremely subtle film grain overlay
* quiet click sounds inspired by premium camera shutters or vintage editorial interfaces (optional and user-toggleable)

## Technical quality

120fps smooth scrolling

No layout shifts

Fast loading

Responsive

Keyboard accessible

Everything should feel crafted rather than generated.

---

One last thing.

**Don't tell Replit what to build. Tell it how you want people to feel.**

Instead of saying:

> "Make the text fade in."

Say:

> **"Every interaction should feel like turning a page in a beautifully printed creative journal."**

Good AI tools are much better at translating a feeling into a coherent design language than they are at following a long checklist of visual effects.
